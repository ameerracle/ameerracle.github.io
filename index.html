Welcome to My Personal Webpage

My name is Ameer Nizami. I am a doctoral candidate in the department of Chemical and Materials Engineering at Concordia University. My research integrates an experimental and computational approach to develop lithium-sulfur battery (LSB) cathodes. LSBs are a promising next-generation energy storage technology, offering significantly higher capacity, lower cost, and improved environmental sustainability compared to conventional lithium-ion batteries. By leveraging computational chemistry and artificial intelligence, I aim to synthesize novel materials for enhancing the cathode interface. I am also a 2024 Public Scholar, where I strive to present my research to the general public. My doctoral research is generously funded by the NSERC PGS-D scholarship and Concordia (Doctoral Split Fellowship and Professor Hugh McQueen Award of Excellence).

My extracurriculars

Outside of academia, I am an an active member of the Chemical Institute of Canada, Montreal section. I have regularly participated in public speaking events, such as Concordia's 3MT competition or AQPER. I enjoy promoting sustainable energy and storage solutions. Recently, I have co-founded an AI cluster for materials and molecules under Concordia's Applied AI institute. We hope to bring the knowledge and tools to the Concordia and Montreal computational chemistry community!
