My Artwork

Harmony in a Ball Pit: A Molecular Ballet

The image represents a potential metal candidate that can improve the reliability of the lithium-sulfur battery cathode. To identify the ideal coating material, the candidates are screened using computational modeling. One such example is the optimized titanium nitride structure, represented by the silver and blue spheres. The lithium sulfide adsorbate molecules, shown in purple and yellow, dance as energy is released and stored during the charging and discharging processes. They play a key role by binding to the coating. My theoretical and experimental research focuses on enhancing this interaction, which can lead to improved charging rates and increased battery capacity. This multidisciplinary approach will lead batteries to become a more reliable energy storage device.
